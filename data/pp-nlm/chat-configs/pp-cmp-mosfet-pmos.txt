KEY FACTS

**Why P-channel exists:**
- For HIGH-SIDE switching of a load to VCC. (NMOS does low-side: load to NMOS to GND.)
- Gate must go BELOW source by |VGS(th)| to turn on. Source is typically tied to VCC.
- For an MCU to turn on a PMOS at VCC=12V, the gate needs to be pulled to ~0V — usually via an NMOS pull-down.
- A bare 3.3V GPIO can't directly turn off a 12V-source PMOS; the GPIO must drive an NMOS that pulls the PMOS gate down.

**Reverse-polarity protection idiom:**
- PMOS in series with the supply, source toward load, drain toward VCC, gate to GND.
- Forward connection: gate is below source by VCC → PMOS conducts via channel (lower drop than the body diode).
- Reverse connection: body diode is reverse-biased → no current flows. Battery saved.
- Lower forward drop than a Schottky diode for low-VCC battery applications.

**High-side load switch:**
- Source → VCC. Drain → load. Load → GND.
- Gate driven by NMOS pull-down: NMOS off → gate pulled to VCC by 10kΩ pull-up → PMOS off. NMOS on → gate dragged to GND → PMOS fully on.
- Inverted-logic: MCU "1" → load OFF; MCU "0" → load ON. Account for this in firmware OR add an inverter.

**Common parts to research (when used in projects):**
- Si2301 (SOT-23, ~3A, logic-level VGS(th)~−1V) — small SMD high-side switch.
- AO3401 (SOT-23, ~4A, logic-level) — slightly higher current.
- IRLML6402 (SOT-23, ~3.7A, logic-level VGS(th)~−1V) — very common in dev-board reverse-polarity sections.
- IRF9540N (TO-220, 19A, STANDARD level VGS(th)~−4V) — power P-MOSFET; needs ≥10V gate drop to fully enhance.
- DMP3098L (SOT-23, ~3.6A, logic-level) — alternative to AO3401.

**Pitfalls:**
- Polarity confusion. PMOS gate drive is INVERTED relative to NMOS — easiest source of bugs.
- Floating gate hazard. Same as N-MOSFET — pull the gate to source via 10kΩ to ensure off-state at boot. For high-side configurations, that's gate-to-VCC.
- VGS(max) limit. PMOS can be damaged if gate is dragged too far below source (often max −20V). When source = 24V, do NOT drive gate to GND directly without verifying VGS(max).
- RDS(on) is typically 2-3× higher than equivalent NMOS due to hole mobility — worse for high-current.

**For PWM dimming high-side:**
- PWM the NMOS gate driver, not the PMOS gate directly (CGS×Rgate timing matters).
- Use a dedicated high-side gate driver IC (TPS22918, FPF1320, or similar load-switch ICs) for clean PWM at >10kHz.
