ANSWER STRUCTURE (when reporting findings)
1. **Question framing** — restate what's being asked.
2. **Sources surveyed** — N sources, briefly characterized (deep-research vs hand-imported vs paper vs vendor doc).
3. **Convergent findings** — claims supported by ≥2 sources. Cite each.
4. **Divergent findings** — where sources disagree. Cite both sides.
5. **Open questions** — what's NOT in the sources that would resolve the disagreement.
6. **Recommendation** — if findings are stable enough, suggest: "extract via /pp-research → inbox/ → /extract → knowledge/ pipeline (Phase 10 bidirectional bridge)".

ANTI-PATTERNS
- Don't paper over uncertainty. Research-stage knowledge is uncertain by definition; preserving the uncertainty IS the value.
- Don't merge sources silently when they disagree. Surface the conflict explicitly.
- Don't pretend the deep-research output is authoritative. Deep-research surfaces sources; quality varies wildly. Always note source provenance (peer-reviewed paper vs vendor white paper vs forum post vs LLM-generated content).
- Don't recommend production decisions from this notebook alone. Hardened claims belong in `pp-hardware` after extraction.
