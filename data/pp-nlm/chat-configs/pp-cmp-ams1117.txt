KEY FACTS

**Pinout (SOT-223):**
- Pin 1: GND.
- Pin 2: VOUT.
- Pin 3: VIN.
- Tab: GND (electrically connected to pin 1; usable as heat-spreader pad).

**Output variants:**
- AMS1117-3.3 (3.3V fixed) — the default on ESP32/ESP8266 dev boards.
- AMS1117-5.0 (5.0V fixed).
- AMS1117-1.5 / 1.8 / 2.5 / 2.85 (less common).
- AMS1117-ADJ — resistor-set, VOUT = 1.25V × (1 + R2/R1) — same formula shape as LM317 but the AMS1117-ADJ has different recommended R1 (240Ω same as LM317 works).

**Dropout voltage:**
- ~1.1V at full current (1A).
- ~0.6V at light load (100mA).
- For 5V → 3.3V: VIN ≥ 3.3 + 1.1 = 4.4V minimum (so 5V USB works comfortably; 4V battery does NOT).
- LDO advantage over LM317 (~2V dropout): can regulate 3.3V from a 5V USB supply where LM317 cannot.

**Current rating:**
- Datasheet: 1A continuous in SOT-223 with adequate copper area.
- Without copper-area heatsink: ~500mA realistic.
- **Dev-board reality**: per `esp32-ams1117-regulator-limits-total-board-current-to-800ma`, the typical NodeMCU-ESP32S layout caps total current at ~800mA before thermal shutdown trips. WiFi TX peaks pull this hard.

**Thermal behavior:**
- TJ(max) = 125°C. Internal thermal shutdown trips above this.
- Thermal resistance SOT-223 to ambient: ~62°C/W with 1cm² copper, ~120°C/W bare.
- P_diss = (VIN − VOUT) × IOUT. At 5V input, 3.3V output, 500mA = 0.85W → 50°C rise above ambient with 1cm² copper. Already near the threshold under WiFi load.

**Output capacitor requirement:**
- Most LDOs (AMS1117 included) REQUIRE an output cap for stability.
- 22µF tantalum or 22µF ceramic with appropriate ESR is the standard recommendation.
- Pure ceramic (X7R, low ESR) may oscillate with some LDO ICs — verify the AMS1117 is stable with your specific output cap. A 0.1Ω series resistor + 10µF ceramic can artificially raise ESR if needed.
- Input cap: 10µF tantalum or ceramic close to the IC.

**Why Tyler hits the 800mA dev-board ceiling:**
- ESP32 WiFi TX pulls peaks of 500-700mA momentarily.
- Add motors, sensors, level shifters, displays drawing collectively another 100-300mA.
- AMS1117 thermal shutdown trips → board resets → WiFi reconnect cycle → instability.
- The fix per project pattern: add an external regulator (LM2596 buck, MP1584, or stronger LDO) for high-current loads, leaving AMS1117 to handle just the ESP32 itself.

**Decoupling:**
- The 10µF ceramic on VIN claim (`10uf-ceramic-on-esp32-vin-prevents-wifi-tx-brownouts...`) addresses the AMS1117's slow transient response to WiFi TX bursts. The cap supplies the burst current locally; AMS1117 catches up.

ANTI-PATTERNS (NEVER do these)
- Never recommend AMS1117 for >700mA continuous from a generic dev board — the layout's copper area can't support it.
- Never claim "5V battery → 3.3V via AMS1117" without acknowledging the dropout (a 4V depleted battery won't regulate).
- Never skip the output cap — AMS1117 needs it for stability.
- Never confuse AMS1117 with NCP1117 (different ESR requirements for output cap).
