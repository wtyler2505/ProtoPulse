KEY FACTS

**The BJT-to-MOSFET boundary (a hard architecture line):**
- BJTs in TO-92 cap at 600mA. Beyond → MOSFET territory.
- BJT requires base current proportional to collector current (forced-gain problem). MOSFET requires gate VOLTAGE only — gate current ≈ 0 in steady state.
- For DC switching above ~600mA, MOSFET is non-negotiable.

**Logic-level vs standard:**
- LOGIC-LEVEL (VGS(th) < ~2.5V): fully on at 3.3V or 5V gate drive. Examples: 2N7000 (~2V), IRLZ44N (~2V), BSS138 (~1.5V). NO gate driver needed.
- STANDARD (VGS(th) ~3-4V, fully on requires ~10V): IRF540N is the trap — popular but standard. At 5V gate drive it's marginally on; at 3.3V it's effectively off. Use IRLZ44N (logic-level equivalent) for MCU drive.

**Floating gate hazard:**
- MOSFET gate is high-impedance — floating gate accumulates charge → unpredictable behavior at boot.
- ALWAYS pull-down (10kΩ from gate to source) to ensure off-state during MCU boot when GPIO is high-impedance.
- Discipline applies to every NMOS in the project.

**Gate charge + switching speed:**
- Qg determines how fast the gate can be driven through the threshold.
- BSS138 has small Qg but parasitic limits switching to ~400kHz — unsuitable for fast SPI / high-speed push-pull. Use a faster part (2N7002 SOT-23 has higher speed) or dedicated gate driver IC for that range.

**Common parts in Tyler's projects:**
- **2N7000** (TO-92, 200mA, VGS(th)~2V) — small-signal logic-level. Switch ID, FET op-amps, simple level shift.
- **BSS138** (SOT-23, 200mA, VGS(th)~1.5V) — surface-mount logic-level. Common in level-shifter modules. **400kHz speed cap.**
- **IRLZ44N** (TO-220, 47A, VGS(th)~2V, RDS(on)~22mΩ) — logic-level POWER MOSFET. Tyler's go-to for high-current MCU-driven loads.
- **IRF540N** (TO-220, 33A, VGS(th)~3.5V, RDS(on)~44mΩ) — STANDARD level — needs ≥10V gate drive. **TRAP: popular for legacy reasons but doesn't fully enhance from MCU GPIO.**

**Body diode:**
- Every MOSFET has an intrinsic body diode (drain-source).
- Useful for inductive load freewheeling but slow recovery — not suitable as a deliberate fast diode.
- **BLDC + dynamic brake hazards** (`bldc-direction-reversal-under-load-creates-destructive-current-spikes-through-mosfets`, `dynamic-brake-must-be-pulsed-not-held-because-stationary-phase-shorting-overheats-mosfets`) — surface explicitly when motor switching topology is the question.

**Heat:**
- P_diss = ID² × RDS(on). At 5A through 22mΩ = 550mW. TO-220 needs heatsink above ~1W continuous.
- Watch the saturation region. If gate drive is marginal, MOSFET runs in linear region with much higher dissipation.

ANTI-PATTERNS (NEVER do these)
- Never recommend IRF540N for MCU-driven switching without a gate driver. It's standard, not logic-level.
- Never leave the gate floating. Pull-down is mandatory.
- Never switch BLDC under load without surfacing the destructive current spike claim.
- Never recommend BSS138 for >400kHz signals. It's a level-shifter, not a fast switch.
