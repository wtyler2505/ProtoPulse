KEY FACTS

**The output formula:**
- `VOUT = 1.25V √ó (1 + R2/R1) + IADJ √ó R2`
- `IADJ ‚âà 50¬µA` (small, often ignored unless R2 is huge).
- Practical: pick R1 = 240Œ© (datasheet recommendation ‚Äî keeps minimum load current ‚âà 5mA which the LM317 needs to regulate). Then R2 = 240Œ© √ó (VOUT/1.25 ‚àí 1).
- Examples: VOUT=5V ‚Üí R2=720Œ©. VOUT=9V ‚Üí R2=1488Œ©. VOUT=12V ‚Üí R2=2064Œ©.

**Heat dissipation reality (the cliff):**
- `P_diss = (VIN ‚àí VOUT) √ó IOUT` ‚Äî quote the vault claim.
- Example: VIN=12V, VOUT=5V, IOUT=500mA ‚Üí P_diss = 7V √ó 0.5A = 3.5W. TO-220 unheatsinked = ~62¬∞C/W ‚Üí +217¬∞C above ambient = thermal shutdown / destruction.
- High differential (e.g., 12V‚Üí3.3V at 1A = 8.7W) is DANGEROUS without a substantial heatsink. Switch to a buck converter for those.
- Always do the heat math BEFORE the layout.

**Dropout voltage:**
- ~1.5-2V at full current. VIN must be at least VOUT + 2V for regulation.
- Example: 5V output requires VIN ‚â• 7V. Cannot regulate 5V from a 6V supply with LM317 ‚Äî use an LDO (AMS1117 has ~1V dropout) or buck instead.

**Pinout (TO-220):**
- Pin 1: ADJ (sets output via R1/R2 divider).
- Pin 2: OUT (regulated output).
- Pin 3: IN (raw input).
- TAB is connected to OUT (NOT GND like LM7805) ‚Äî heatsink must be electrically isolated OR float at VOUT potential.

**Current limit:**
- Internal current limit ~2.2A typical, foldback to ~0.5A under thermal stress.
- Datasheet rates 1.5A continuous with adequate cooling.

**ADJ pin behavior:**
- The ADJ pin is essentially the inverting input of an internal error amplifier referenced to OUT ‚àí 1.25V.
- For ripple rejection, add a 10¬µF cap from ADJ to GND (bypasses ripple injected via R2).
- Typical input/output caps: 10¬µF on input (close to pin), 10¬µF tantalum or low-ESR ceramic on output.

**Protection diodes:**
- For VOUT > 25V: add a reverse diode from OUT to IN (1N4002) to protect during input short circuits.
- For ADJ-bypass-capacitor protection: add a reverse diode from ADJ to OUT.

**LM337 (negative rail twin):**
- Same formula but with negative voltages: `VOUT = ‚àí1.25V √ó (1 + R2/R1)`.
- Pinout differs: ADJ-OUT-IN vs LM317's IN-OUT-ADJ ‚Äî DOUBLE-CHECK before placement.
- Bipolar (split) supplies use LM317 + LM337 paired off a center-tapped transformer.

**When to use LM317 vs alternatives:**
- LM317: variable output, simple, low parts count, available everywhere. **Heat budget must allow it.**
- LM7805 (fixed 5V): cheaper, lower parts count when 5V is needed. Same heat math.
- AMS1117-3.3 (LDO): when dropout < 2V matters (5V ‚Üí 3.3V from USB). Lower current capability (~800mA). See `pp-cmp-ams1117`.
- Buck converter (LM2596, MP1584, etc.): when efficiency matters (P_diss = 0.85 √ó VIN √ó IOUT regardless of differential ‚Äî but with switching noise).

ANTI-PATTERNS (NEVER do these)
- Never recommend LM317 for >2V differential at >500mA without surfacing the heatsink requirement.
- Never assume the TAB is GND ‚Äî it's OUT. Isolate from chassis.
- Never use LM317 for a 5V ‚Üí 3.3V drop (LDO territory; AMS1117 dropout is much smaller).
- Never skip the minimum load resistor (R1=240Œ©) ‚Äî without it, the regulator may not maintain regulation under no-load.
