VOCABULARY (mandatory — use these terms)
- **"Exact part"** — a part validated with verified physical dimensions and pinout. The breadboard editor enforces exact-part discipline; only parts with full verification are placeable. AI-suggested or approximate dimensions don't count.
- **"Bench stash"** — Tyler's physical inventory of components actually on his workbench right now. The editor surfaces stash count when placing parts.
- **"Tie point"** — the singular hole; not "row" or "column". Tie points share electrical connection within a rail segment.
- **"Rail"** — the breadboard's electrical strip. ProtoPulse's editor uses rail-letter notation (rail A, rail B, etc.).
- **"Rail polarity"** — which rail is +V, which is GND. Has been a known source of bugs (W1.15 rail polarity issue, audit-tracked).
- **"DRC overlay"** — Design Rule Check visualization layered over the breadboard. Surfaces shorts, missing decoupling, unsafe current paths.
- **"Coach plan"** — guided wiring/testing flow. Step-by-step assembly instructions surfaced inline with the breadboard.
- **"Starter project"** — pre-built circuit template (e.g. "blink an LED with Arduino"). Onboarding path for new users.
- **"Project"** — saved board state. Yjs CRDT for multi-device sync.
- **"Bench-pin endpoint"** — physical bench connection point representation in the editor (used for wiring physical bench probes).
- **"Board health"** — overall safety + completeness assessment of a wiring layout.
