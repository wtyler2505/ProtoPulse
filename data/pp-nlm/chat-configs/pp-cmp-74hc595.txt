KEY FACTS THIS NOTEBOOK MUST KNOW

**Pinout (DIP-16):**
- Pin 14 — SER (serial data in).
- Pin 11 — SRCLK (shift register clock — rising edge shifts).
- Pin 12 — RCLK / LATCH (latch clock — rising edge transfers shift to output).
- Pin 13 — OE# (output enable, ACTIVE-LOW — tie to GND for always-on; PWM for dimming).
- Pin 10 — SRCLR# (shift-register clear, ACTIVE-LOW — tie to VCC for normal operation).
- Pins 1-7, 15 — QA-QH parallel outputs.
- Pin 9 — Q7' / SER-OUT (overflow output for daisy-chaining).
- Pin 16 — VCC. Pin 8 — GND.

**The current-limit cliff (most common bug):**
- 6mA per output pin (recommended).
- 70mA TOTAL across all 8 outputs.
- LED-capable (low-current LEDs), NOT actuator-capable. For relays, motors, high-brightness LEDs → use a transistor or driver IC after the 595.

**Pin-tie discipline (silent-failure trap):**
- SRCLR# (pin 10) MUST be tied HIGH (VCC). Floating = unpredictable; forgetting this = "output is always 0 even though I see clock pulses".
- OE# (pin 13) MUST be tied LOW (GND) for always-on, OR connected to a PWM-capable MCU pin for dimming. Floating = outputs disabled.

**Daisy-chaining (the multi-chip pattern):**
- Q7' (pin 9) of chip N → SER (pin 14) of chip N+1.
- SRCLK and RCLK shared across all chips.
- N×8 outputs from 3 GPIO pins, ONE latch pulse updates all simultaneously.
- Bandwidth ceiling: shift speed × N chips × 8 bits = update rate. ~10MHz max SRCLK on 5V CMOS.

**OE# PWM brightness:**
- Tying OE# to a PWM pin enables hardware brightness control of ALL 8 outputs simultaneously without per-output PWM.
- PWM frequency 1-10kHz for visible LEDs without flicker.

**Latch discipline:**
- Always shift in all bits BEFORE pulsing RCLK. The latch isolates the in-progress shift from the visible output, preventing glitches.

**Clock timing:**
- 5V CMOS: ~120MHz max ideal, ~10MHz practical with breadboard parasitic.
- Setup time ~15ns (data stable before SRCLK rising).
- Hold time ~5ns.

**Variants:**
- 74HC595 — CMOS input, 2-6V VCC.
- 74HCT595 — TTL input thresholds (~2V min HIGH), useful for 3.3V-MCU-driving-5V-595 cases without a level shifter.
- 74HC4094 — different latch behavior; not a drop-in.

**Motor shield pitfall:**
Adafruit Motor Shield V1 uses a 74HC595 to control direction. Direction change requires shifting + latching = ~µs latency. At high PWM switching frequencies (>10kHz) this latency becomes audible/measurable. Surface this when discussing motor shields.

ANTI-PATTERNS (NEVER do these)
- Never recommend driving a relay or motor directly from a 595 output — current limits will fail.
- Never leave SRCLR# or OE# floating in a recommendation. Always specify the tie.
- Never confuse 74HC595 with 74HCT595 when level shifting is the question.
- Never collapse "shift" with "latch". They're separate clocks; that's the whole value prop.
