Functional Analysis and Operating Principles

MEMS Microphone Principle: The SPH0645LM4H is a Microelectromechanical System (MEMS) microphone. It consists of a sensitive diaphragm and a pre-amplifier built into a single, small package. The pre-amplifier converts the analog sound wave into a digital signal using a sigma-delta modulator.

Pulse Density Modulation (PDM): PDM is a form of digital audio encoding where the signal's information is encoded in the density of pulses (high/low transitions). The signal's amplitude is represented by the density of high pulses over time. This high-frequency, single-bit stream is efficient for digital transmission over short distances.

I2S-Compatible Output: While technically PDM, the signal output from this microphone is often processed by an I2S (Inter-IC Sound) peripheral on the host MCU. The host MCU's I2S peripheral contains a hardware decimation filter. This filter converts the high-frequency PDM stream into a lower-rate, higher-resolution (e.g., 16-bit or 18-bit) PCM (Pulse Code Modulation) stream, which is the standard format for digital audio processing. The LRCLK (Word Select) and BCLK (Bit Clock) signals synchronize this process.

Stereo Configuration: The SEL (Channel Select) pin allows two identical PDM microphones to share a single I2S bus. By connecting one mic's SEL pin to ground (Left channel) and the other mic's SEL pin to VCC (Right channel), the microphones will time-multiplex their data onto the single DOUT line, allowing the host MCU to capture a stereo audio stream.

Functional Analysis and Operating Principles

A "floating" connector utilizes a spring-loaded or sliding mechanism within its housing to allow lateral movement of the contact block relative to the fixed solder pads on the PCB[6]. This mechanical compliance is crucial for high-density applications where small misalignments, often caused by thermal expansion or manufacturing tolerances, would otherwise lead to high stress on the solder joints[6].

In the case of the B322 series, the floating mechanism enables a "self-alignment" feature. When the plug (header) and receptacle mate, the floating action allows for a tolerance of ±0.60 mm in the X-Y plane[9]. This prevents a phenomenon known as "solder joint fatigue," where continuous stress from misalignment causes microscopic cracks in the solder, eventually leading to intermittent connection failures.
