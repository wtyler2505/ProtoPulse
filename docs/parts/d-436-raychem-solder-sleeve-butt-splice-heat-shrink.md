# D-436 Raychem Solder Sleeve Butt Splice Heat Shrink

Aerospace-grade wire splice from Raychem (now TE Connectivity). This is a heat-shrink sleeve with a solder preform ring embedded inside — apply heat and the sleeve shrinks to seal the splice while the solder ring melts and flows to create the electrical connection. One step, one tool (heat gun), and you get a soldered, insulated, strain-relieved splice that's rated for extreme environments.

## How It Works

The solder sleeve has three functional zones:

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────┐
│  Heat-shrink   │ Solder ring │ Heat-shrink  │
│  insulation    │ + flux      │ insulation   │
└─────────────────────────────────────────┘
       ↑              ↑              ↑
   Seals left     Solders the     Seals right
   wire entry     connection      wire entry
```

1. **Slide** the sleeve over one wire before stripping.
2. **Strip** both wire ends and overlap them inside the sleeve, centered on the solder ring.
3. **Apply heat** uniformly with a heat gun (200-300°C). The sleeve shrinks, the flux activates, and the solder melts and wicks into the stranded wire.
4. **Cool** — you now have a soldered, insulated, environmentally sealed splice.

## Application Notes

- **Heat evenly** — rotate the sleeve or move the heat gun around it. Uneven heating can cause the solder to flow to one side or the sleeve to shrink unevenly.
- **Don't overheat** — once the sleeve has fully shrunk and you see solder flow (a small ring of solder may be visible at the edges), remove heat. Continued heating can damage the insulation.
- **Wire prep matters** — clean, oxide-free wire ends produce the best solder joints. The integrated flux helps, but heavily oxidized or contaminated wire will still give a poor connection.
- **Test the joint** — after cooling, tug-test the splice. A properly soldered sleeve joint should be as strong as the wire itself.

## Why These Are Special

Raychem solder sleeves are mil-spec components used in aerospace, military, and high-reliability applications. They produce a splice that is:

- **Soldered** — not just crimped. Actual metallurgical bond.
- **Sealed** — the heat-shrink provides environmental protection against moisture and contaminants.
- **Consistent** — less operator-dependent than manual soldering. The solder preform ensures the right amount of solder every time.
- **Inspectable** — the clear or translucent sleeve lets you visually verify solder flow.

In hobby projects, these are overkill — but they're also the best possible wire splice you can make, and they're faster than separate solder + heat shrink steps.
